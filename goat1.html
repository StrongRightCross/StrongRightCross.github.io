Goats And Penguins

An Alaskan fisherman falls into the great cold blue. No one is awake to throw a rope overboard. Biting back the chill, he swims to the only refuge in sight, a small chunk of ice floating out into nowhere. After a short but freezing swim, he reaches his destination, but the temperatures have taken their toll and the man's hands are too numb to climb ashore the natural life raft. Accepting his destiny, the fisherman lets go of the cold ice. But just as he begins to drift downwards, he feels something from below, and instead of sinking is now being propelled upwards. The man climbs aboard the sheet of ice, and quickly turns to look to the instrument of his rescue. He hears only a small "bah" in the distance.


Does this story sound strange to you? I can understand if it does. In fact it might have sounded strange to me at one time too. But let me share with you what I have found. It won't take long, and soon you too will see just how very much goat is a penguin, and how much penguin is a goat.

Archaelogical Evidence
If goats and penguins are truly the same creature, would not humanity have stumbled upon this revelation in the past? Humans have been around for a very long time after all. How can we truly explain no one realizing the two are one and the same creature? Well, if we look closer at the evidence, we shall see that this observation is not a modern one. Take for instance the people groups inhabbiting the ancient mid east. Out of all the many languages that have come and gone in the mid east in the past few thousand years, not a single one has had a word for penguin. Are we to think that people simply forgot to name penguins? That every other animal has a word to describe them but penguins do not? This is absurd! There is no word in the ancient mid east for penguin, but there is one for goat. Doesn't it make more sense to say they used the same word for the one animal than there are two animals and only one got a name over several milenia?
